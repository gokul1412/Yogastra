5 Simple Steps to Start a Morning Meditation Practice

May 12, 2026 Kiran Rani 5 Min Read

Starting your day with just 10 minutes of meditation can completely shift your mindset. Here is how to build a consistent habit.

1. Start Small

Don't try to sit for an hour on your first day. Start with just 5 to 10 minutes. The goal is consistency, not duration. As the habit forms, you can gradually increase the time.

2. Create a Dedicated Space

Having a specific spot where you meditate signals to your brain that it's time to relax. It doesn't need to be an entire room—a corner with a comfortable cushion and maybe a candle is perfect.

"Meditation is not evasion; it is a serene encounter with reality."

Thích Nhất Hạnh

3. Focus on the Breath

When you sit, simply observe your breath. Notice the sensation of air entering and leaving your nostrils. When your mind wanders (and it will), gently bring your focus back to the breath without judgment.

Remember, the goal is not to clear your mind entirely, but to become aware of your thoughts without getting caught up in them.
